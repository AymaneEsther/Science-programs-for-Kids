Biology

Studying biology can help students gain a deeper appreciation for the natural world and prepare them for future academic and career pursuits. It also helps students develop critical thinking skills and understand important concepts such as genetics, evolution, and ecology.

Biology programs available for low-income high school kids in Philadelphia.

Penn Summer Science Academy

The University of Pennsylvania offers the Penn Summer Science Academy, which provides hands-on laboratory experience in biology and other sciences. The program is free for students who qualify for free or reduced lunch.

Philadelphia Science Festival

The Philadelphia Science Festival hosts a variety of events and activities throughout the city to promote science education. The festival includes workshops, exhibits, and demonstrations in biology and other sciences, and is free and open to the public.

Franklin Institute

The Franklin Institute offers several science programs for high school students, including the Discovery Camp and STEM Scholars Program. These programs provide opportunities for students to explore biology and other sciences through hands-on learning experiences and mentorship from professionals in the field.
